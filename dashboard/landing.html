Honest answers

Questions people ask
before they book the call.

"Will it actually bring in clients?"

It depends on your offer and how you show up. Podcast listeners are warm and pre-sold on you before they ever reach out. But if your offer isn't clear or you're not ready to make an ask, no channel fixes that. If you've got something worth selling and you can speak to it well, this works.

"Will the pitches sound like me?"

Yes. Every pitch is written from your profile, in your voice, for that specific host. You review it before it goes anywhere. Edit it, rewrite it, or approve it as-is. Nothing sends without your say-so. Most clients say they'd have written it the same way.

"I don't have time to do podcasts."

Honestly, this works best for people who see growing their business as a priority worth protecting time for. We handle all the logistics. But if speaking to new audiences isn't something you'd carve out time for right now, this probably isn't the right moment. No hard sell here.

"How is this better than paid ads?"

It's not a replacement, it's a different game. Ads buy attention. Podcasts build trust. If you're already running ads, this adds a channel that warms the same audience in a way paid traffic never does. Most clients run both and find the podcast listeners close faster.

"Why now? My business is already growing."

Great. This is designed for businesses that are already moving and want to add a compounding channel on top of what's working. If growth feels comfortable where it is and you're not looking to accelerate, that's completely fine. This is for the ones who want more and are ready to act on it.

"What if hosts don't reply?"

They often don't on the first email. That's normal. That's why automatic follow-ups go out at day 7. Most bookings happen on the second touch. And the pipeline keeps running, with new shows added every session so momentum never stops.

"I don't have a big following. Will hosts actually want to book me?"

Most podcast hosts care more about your story and what you can give their audience than your follower count. We match you to shows where your expertise — not your audience size — is the draw. Many of our clients booked their first show with zero social following.
