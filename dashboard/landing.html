Honest answers

Questions people ask
before they book the call.

"Will it actually bring in clients?"

It depends on your offer and how you show up. Podcast listeners are warm and pre-sold on you before they ever reach out. But if your offer isn't clear or you're not ready to make an ask, no channel fixes that. If you've got something worth selling and you can speak to it well, this works.

"I don't have time to do podcasts."

Honestly, this works best for people who see growing their business as a priority worth protecting time for. We handle all the logistics. But if speaking to new audiences isn't something you'd carve out time for right now, this probably isn't the right moment. No hard sell here.

"How is this better than paid ads?"

It's not a replacement, it's a different game. Ads buy attention. Podcasts build trust. If you're already running ads, this adds a channel that warms the same audience in a way paid traffic never does. If you want a straight swap, this probably isn't the right frame for you.

"Why now? My business is already growing."

Great. This is designed for businesses that are already moving and want to add a compounding channel on top of what's working. If growth feels comfortable where it is and you're not looking to accelerate, that's completely fine. This is for the ones who want more and are ready to act on it.
