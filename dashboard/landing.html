Honest answers

Questions people ask
before they book the call.

"I don't have a big following. Will hosts book me?"

Most hosts care about your story and what you give their audience. Not your follower count. We match you to shows where your expertise, not your audience size, is the draw. Many clients booked their first show with zero social following.

"How can you guarantee bookings if you can't control the hosts?"

We don't control hosts. We control volume, quality, and follow-up. Our database has 4M+ podcasts refreshed weekly. We personalize every pitch (no templates). We follow up relentlessly. Most replies come from follow-up two through five, not the first email. We don't pitch 10 shows and hope. We pitch 50 to 100 until we hit the guarantee. If we miss, we keep pitching at no extra cost. You don't pay the final 50% until every booking is confirmed, so we eat the risk, not you.

"I don't have time for 12 recordings."

Total time investment: about 2 hours per week. One hour for a recording (some weeks two, some weeks zero). One hour to review and approve content assets. We handle research, pitching, booking coordination, prep sheets, and content production. If you can't commit two hours per week to becoming the authority in your space, this isn't for you.

"What if I freeze or bomb on the show?"

We send a prep sheet 48 hours before every recording: talking points, episode angle, host background, three to five stories to tell. You're never walking in blind. Podcast hosts are professional interviewers. They ask questions, you answer. If you can talk about your work for 45 minutes with a client, you can do this. 95% of clients say their first recording was easier than they thought.

"What type of shows can you actually get me on?"

We match you to shows your ideal clients listen to, regardless of size. Small targeted shows (500 to 5,000 downloads) often convert better than huge ones because the audience is niche-specific. Medium shows (5,000 to 50,000) are the sweet spot. We pitch the big ones too when your positioning is strong. The filter is never size. The filter is whether the host's audience contains your ideal clients.

"What's the difference between this and other booking services?"

Most are transactional. They spray 500 generic pitches, hope for five bookings, and disappear. We're a production company. We build your Story Arc, write personalized pitches that make hosts say "who IS this person", run the funnel, produce the content, and don't stop until every booking is confirmed. Competitors book you on shows and leave. We book you, solve your content problem for a year, and give you a system that keeps compounding.
