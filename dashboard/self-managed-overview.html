The Problem
Doing this properly by yourself is a full-time job.
Finding shows takes forever
Searching directories, checking if shows are still active, verifying they take guests, finding a contact email. Most people spend a full day to find three decent shows. Then they do nothing with them.
Writing pitches is a grind
A good pitch takes 20 to 30 minutes per show. You have to listen to recent episodes, understand the audience, write something that does not sound like a template, and still sound like yourself. It is exhausting to do at scale.
Nobody follows up
Most bookings come from the second or third contact. Hosts are busy. They miss emails. A polite follow-up a week later is often all it takes. Almost nobody sends it because they forget or feel awkward.
