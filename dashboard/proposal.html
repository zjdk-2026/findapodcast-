Common Questions

The real objections.
The real answers.

How do you guarantee bookings if you can't control the hosts?
We don't control hosts. We control volume, quality, and follow-up. Our database has 4M+ podcasts refreshed weekly. We personalize every pitch — not templates sent to 500 shows. We follow up relentlessly. Most bookings come from follow-up 2, 3, or 5 — not the first email. We don't pitch 10 shows and hope. We pitch 50 to 100 until we hit the guarantee. If we miss 6 months, we keep working at no extra charge. We own the risk, not you.
I'm not a big name. Will hosts actually want me?
Good. That's exactly who this works for. 90% of our clients are invisible experts — great results, zero reach. Hosts don't book followers. They book stories. Your origin arc, contrarian belief, and client transformation proof — that's what gets you booked. We've turned complete unknowns into podcast authorities. The Story Arc positioning makes you interesting whether you have 500 followers or 50,000. Follower count is a vanity metric. A compelling story is a booking.
I don't have time for 12 recordings.
Total time investment: 2 hours per week average. One hour for one podcast recording (some weeks two, some weeks zero). One hour to review and approve content assets. That's it. We handle research (10 to 20 hours per week), pitching (15 to 30 hours per week), booking coordination, prep sheets, and content production. You show up, talk about your work, and approve the output. If you can't commit 2 hours per week to becoming the authority in your space, this isn't for you.
What if I go on 12 shows and get zero clients?
Two scenarios. Scenario 1: The shows didn't deliver listeners. That means we failed — wrong shows, wrong audience, wrong positioning. We owe you replacement bookings until the guarantee is met with better matched shows. Scenario 2: The shows delivered listeners but your offer or funnel didn't convert. That's a conversion problem, not a visibility problem. We include sales funnel optimization and lead magnet creation in the Tour package specifically to reduce this risk. 80% of podcast listeners have bought from host recommendations (Edison Research). If you're on the right shows with the right message and a working funnel, leads will come.
What if I freeze or bomb on the show? I'm not confident on camera.
Most clients feel this way before recording number one. Here's what actually happens: We send you a prep sheet 48 hours before — talking points, episode angle, host background, 3 to 5 stories to tell. You're never walking in blind. Podcast hosts are professional interviewers. They ask questions. You answer. It's a conversation, not a speech. If you can talk about your work for 45 minutes with a client, you can do this. 95% of clients say the same thing after their first recording: "That was way easier than I thought. I actually enjoyed it." The nerves disappear after recording number one — every time.
What type of shows can you actually get me on?
We match you to shows your ideal clients listen to — regardless of size. Small but targeted shows (500 to 5,000 downloads per episode) often convert better than massive shows because the audience is niche-specific. A leadership coach on a 2,000-download HR podcast gets more leads than on a 50,000-download general business show. We also pitch medium shows (5k–50k) and swing at big ones (50k+) when your positioning is strong. The filter isn't size. The filter is: does this host's audience contain your ideal clients? If yes, we pitch. If no, we skip it even if it's massive. You approve every show before we send a single pitch.
What's the difference between a Podcast Tour and what other booking services do?
Other services — MatchMaker.fm, Interview Valet, Upwork VAs — are transactional. They spray 500 generic pitches, hope for 5 bookings, and disappear. We're a production company. We don't pitch your LinkedIn bio. We build your Story Arc, craft personalized pitches that make hosts say "who IS this person?", produce your content, build your funnel, and keep working until every booking is confirmed. Competitors book you on shows and leave. We book you, eliminate your content creation problem for a year, and give you a system that keeps compounding.
If I sign today, what happens in the next 24 hours?
Immediately: Welcome email, dashboard access, calendar link to book your onboarding call. Day 1: We start reviewing your materials and building your initial target list of 50 to 100 shows. You do nothing. Day 2–7: Onboarding call (30 to 60 min). We finalize your Story Arc, walk through the approved show list, and first pitches go out same day. Week 2–4: First bookings confirmed. Prep sheets sent 48 hours before each recording. The bottom line: You're live on a podcast within 30 days. Guaranteed.
